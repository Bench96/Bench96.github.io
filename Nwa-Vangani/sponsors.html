Sponsors

University of Pretoria - JCP

JCP (Joint Community-Based Project) is conducted by the EBIT Faculty ( Engineering Built Environment and Information Technology) at the University of Pretoria. This project involves students engaging with the community through various community based projects. Nwa-Vangani has collaborated with the University on a number of community-based projects, including the development and redesign of the schoolâ€™s website.

Community Gardening

The community gardening project is an initiative where members of the community can assist in cultivating and maintaining a vegetable garden on the school premises. The produce from this garden is then utilised by the school in multiple ways, including contributing vegetables to a feeding scheme for the learners, selling produce to raise money for the school and some vegetables are taken home by students in order to feed their families.

Hennopspark Laerskool

Our athletes often practice in collaboration with Hennopspark Laerskool athletes. Hennopspark Laerskool often donates books to our library and clothes to our less fortunate learners. Educators from Nwa-Vangani and Hennopspark Laerskool often hold meeting where they share and exchange teaching ideas with one another.

AIDS Awareness Campaign

The AIDS Awareness Campaign is is conducted by the school. The campaign involves parents collaborating with students in creating posters and conducting awareness demonstrations in and around the community. The campaign also covers many aspects including health issues, drugs, abuse, crime prevention and many other socio-economic issues.

Kha Ri Gude Literacy Campaign

The Kha Ri Gude Literacy Campaign is conducted by the Department of Basic Education. The campaign aims to eradicate illiteracy amongst socially disadvantaged people within the community. A number of parents attend classes at the school during the week in the evenings.

Rotary Club

The Rotary Club conducts a breakfast program which is aimed at providing breakfast to school learners who are less fortunate. The Rotary Club provides breakfast to the learners from Monday to Friday. The breakfast is highly nutritions and includes oats, porridge and morvite.
