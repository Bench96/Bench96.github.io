Our Vision

The Invest 4 Africa International Government Field School is a three week program designed to introduce the complete workflow of a governance research project. In the first week, participants design a research survey of relevance to a trade challenges, economic, governance, water-resource investigation, or political study. The design of the course includes considerations of cost and safety and is presented in a competitive manner. The weeklong data-collection phase ensures that participants are familiar with operation of all equipment, including government and public relations, and EM34, and reflection and refraction seismology in mining. Data are processed in the evening, and safety issues are reviewed the following morning. The final week is spent on campus, interpreting and integrating research data to form a coherent model of the sustainable solutions. The participants are selected from three cohorts: University of Ghana political students, Invest 4 Africa Foundation and UNESCO-sponsored African participants, and U. S.-sponsored undergraduates attending as part of a Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU). Instructors include graduate students, resulting in a two-tier training system, as they learn about lecturing, logistics, and field preparation for weeks in advance. The field school has developed a wide-ranging network of geopolitical talent throughout Africa, which is starting to seed new field schools.

In addition, I train undergraduate students.” — Eyob Tesfay, Eritrea
